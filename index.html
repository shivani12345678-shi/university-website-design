Intermediate

Intermediate education is a two-year academic program that students pursue after completing their 10th grade (Matriculation). It includes 11th and 12th grades and serves as a foundation for higher education or professional careers. This level of education is offered by colleges and higher secondary schools.

Degree

A degree is an academic qualification awarded by a university or college after a student completes a specific course of study. Degrees are essential for career advancement, specialization in a field, and professional credibility.

Post Graduation

Postgraduate education refers to the level of study that occurs after completing an undergraduate degree (bachelor's degree). This level of education allows students to specialize in a specific area, conduct advanced research, or develop skills in a professional field.

Our Global Campus

A Global Campus refers to a university or educational institution that operates multiple campuses or learning hubs across different countries or regions, or offers education that can be accessed globally. The concept emphasizes international exposure, global learning, and a diverse student and faculty body.
